# IBIS In Gold – Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

## About IBIS In Gold

**Q: What is IBIS In Gold?**
A: IBIS In Gold is a gold savings platform that allows individuals and businesses to purchase, hold, and manage physical gold as a savings and investment vehicle. Our mission is to make gold ownership simple, accessible, and secure for everyone.

**Q: Where is IBIS In Gold based?**
A: IBIS In Gold operates internationally with customers across multiple countries. Our gold is stored in certified, insured vaults located in Switzerland and Singapore.

**Q: Is IBIS In Gold regulated?**
A: Yes. IBIS In Gold complies with all applicable financial regulations in the jurisdictions we operate. We are audited regularly by independent third parties to ensure transparency and compliance.

---

## Getting Started

**Q: How do I open a gold savings account?**
A: Opening an account is simple:
1. Visit our website and click "Open Account."
2. Complete the online registration form with your personal details.
3. Submit required identity verification documents (KYC/AML process).
4. Once approved, fund your account and start buying gold.

**Q: What documents are required to open an account?**
A: You will need:
- A valid government-issued photo ID (passport or national ID card)
- Proof of address (utility bill or bank statement, dated within 3 months)
- A selfie or video verification may be required for additional identity checks

**Q: How long does account approval take?**
A: Most accounts are approved within 1–2 business days. Complex cases requiring additional verification may take up to 5 business days.

**Q: Is there a minimum deposit to start?**
A: Yes. The minimum initial purchase is equivalent to 1 gram of gold. This ensures every customer can start saving in gold regardless of budget.

## Buying Gold

**Q: How do I buy gold on IBIS In Gold?**
A: Once your account is funded, you can purchase gold directly through the platform:
1. Log in to your account.
2. Go to "Buy Gold."
3. Enter the amount in currency or grams.
4. Confirm the transaction.
Gold is allocated to your account at the live market price at the time of purchase.

**Q: What is the price of gold?**
A: Gold is priced in real-time based on the international spot price (XAU/USD) from global commodity markets. Prices are updated continuously during trading hours. You can view the live price on our platform dashboard.

**Q: Are there any fees when buying gold?**
A: IBIS In Gold charges a small transaction fee of 0.5% on each purchase. There are no hidden fees. Storage fees are included in the annual account maintenance fee.

**Q: Can I buy fractional amounts of gold?**
A: Yes. You can purchase gold in quantities as small as 0.01 grams, allowing you to save incrementally.

**Q: What currencies can I use to buy gold?**
A: We accept payments in USD, EUR, GBP, and several other major currencies. Cryptocurrency payments (BTC, ETH) are also supported on select plans.

## Storing Gold

**Q: Where is my gold stored?**
A: Your gold is stored in fully allocated, segregated vaults in Switzerland and Singapore. This means your gold is physically yours — it is not pooled with other customers' assets.

**Q: Is my gold insured?**
A: Yes. All gold stored through IBIS In Gold is fully insured against theft, loss, and damage through Lloyds of London and other leading insurers.

**Q: Can I take physical delivery of my gold?**
A: Yes. Customers can request physical delivery of their gold in the form of certified gold bars or coins. Delivery fees and minimum quantities apply. Contact our support team for details.

**Q: How do I know my gold is really there?**
A: We provide quarterly audit reports from independent auditors confirming the physical existence and ownership of all gold held on the platform. You can also view your allocated serial numbers in your account dashboard.

## Selling Gold

**Q: How do I sell my gold?**
A: To sell:
1. Log in to your account.
2. Go to "Sell Gold."
3. Enter the amount you wish to sell.
4. Confirm the transaction.
Funds will be credited to your account within 1–2 business days.

**Q: Are there fees for selling gold?**
A: Yes, a transaction fee of 0.5% applies on each sale.

**Q: How quickly can I access my money after selling?**
A: Funds are typically available within 1–2 business days after the sale is confirmed, depending on your bank or payment method.

## Account Management

**Q: What is the annual maintenance fee?**
A: IBIS In Gold charges an annual storage and maintenance fee of 0.15% of the total gold value in your account. This fee covers vault storage, insurance, and account maintenance. It is deducted automatically from your gold balance.

**Q: Can I have multiple accounts?**
A: Each individual may hold one personal account. However, businesses may open a separate corporate account.

**Q: How do I add funds to my account?**
A: You can fund your account via bank transfer (SEPA, SWIFT, or local transfer), credit/debit card, or cryptocurrency. Processing times vary by method.

**Q: What happens if I forget my password?**
A: Click "Forgot Password" on the login page to receive a password reset link to your registered email address.

**Q: Can I set up automatic gold purchases (recurring savings)?**
A: Yes! You can set up a recurring purchase plan — daily, weekly, or monthly — to automate your gold savings strategy.

## Security

**Q: How does IBIS In Gold protect my account?**
A: We use industry-leading security measures including:

**Q: What should I do if I suspect unauthorized activity on my account?**

## Taxes

**Q: Do I need to pay taxes on gold savings?**
A: Tax obligations depend on your country of residence. In many countries, gains from gold investment may be subject to capital gains tax. We recommend consulting a qualified tax advisor in your jurisdiction. IBIS In Gold provides annual statements to help with tax reporting.

**Q: Does IBIS In Gold report my holdings to tax authorities?**
A: We comply with all applicable tax reporting regulations, including CRS (Common Reporting Standard) and FATCA, and may be required to report account information to relevant tax authorities.
